Project Task II--CVS, Eviction, & Larceny in Central Boston: A Casual Exploration

Form Usage and Results

  • For results to be displayed, users should at least select a store in the first selection. Results will be displayed on top of the page after hitting the [Find] button at the end of the form. If you want to check the locations on the GeoMap, before you go the the map page, remember to duplicate the results somewhere either on paper or you devices. The results will be gone after refreshing.
  • The first selection returns a store's address along with its rating and the number of nearby Eviction and Larceny cases.
  • The second selection returns the comparison between second selected store and first selected store. The result will display the vicinities, absolute distance, respective ratings, and their differences in number of nearby Eviction and Larceny cases.
  • The third selection returns the vicinities, total stability and total accessibility of the three stores selected by the three selections. To see the definition of stability and accessibility, please check out task II in the Final Report for detailed explanations.
  • The fourth, fifth, and sixth selections together solve a constraint satisfaction problem we conceived, The K-Salesmen problem. The problem is described as follow: Suppose we want to deploy K salesmen to K stores in our dataset, give the K stores solution that has a total stability greater than or equal to S, and a total accessibility greater than or equal to A. Please check out task II in the Final Report for detailed explanations.
